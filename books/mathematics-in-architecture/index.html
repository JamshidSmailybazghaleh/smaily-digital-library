Book Overview

Mathematics in Architecture explores the hidden mathematical foundations that shape architectural thinking, spatial organization and structural design.

Beginning with the geometric point and extending toward complex spatial systems, the book reveals how mathematics governs proportion, harmony, symmetry, measurement and architectural order.

This publication serves as one of the principal foundational works of the Architecture Domain and establishes the mathematical framework for future architectural studies within the Smaily Knowledge Ecosystem.

Abstract

Mathematics in Architecture investigates the fundamental relationship between mathematical thought and architectural creation.

Through geometry, proportion, symmetry, measurement and spatial reasoning, the book demonstrates that architecture is not merely an artistic discipline but also a structured language governed by mathematical principles.

This publication establishes the mathematical foundation of the Architecture Domain within the Smaily Knowledge Ecosystem and provides a conceptual framework for future architectural research and education.

Author Note

Mathematics is far more than a technical tool for architecture. It is one of the deepest intellectual languages through which order, proportion, structure and beauty become visible.

This publication seeks to reconnect architectural thinking with its mathematical foundations and to demonstrate that every enduring architectural work is ultimately rooted in coherent mathematical relationships.
