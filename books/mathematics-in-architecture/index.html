Mathematics in Architecture

From Point to Infinity

Mathematics • Geometry • Architecture

OVERVIEW

Mathematics in Architecture explores the hidden mathematical foundations that shape architectural thought, design and construction.

From the simplest geometric point to complex spatial systems, the book demonstrates how mathematical principles influence architectural form, proportion, order and beauty.

It presents architecture as a discipline deeply rooted in geometry, logic and structured relationships.

WHY THIS BOOK MATTERS

Architecture and mathematics have been inseparable throughout history. Understanding this relationship provides a deeper appreciation of design, structure and spatial organization.

This work seeks to reconnect contemporary architectural thinking with its mathematical foundations.

AUTHOR NOTE

Mathematics is not merely a technical tool for architecture; it is one of the deepest languages through which architectural order becomes visible.

This book aims to reveal the mathematical structure underlying architectural creation and human perception of space.
